Welcome. I'm Norris, a designer hailing from Northern California. I strive to create things that better society, through care and curiosity.

Currently, I work at Code for America. I focus on improving access to our nation's safety net. We talk to vulnerable populations and the public servants who help them. When we find an opportunity where technology can make a real impact, we pursue it vigoriously.

  1. I lead design on GetCalFresh, a service that has helped millions to apply for food benefits with ease and dignity.
  2. We created a campaign that helped to destigmatize food assistance across college campuses.
  3. I also maintain our accessibility-forward styleguide.

I am fascinated by the creative process, whether it is in the making of chairs, museum exhibits, new technology, typefaces, or public services. Often, I find that it is between those spaces where the magic happens.

  1. Recently, I gave a talk about what chairs can teach us about the safety net.

Previously, I worked at Inkling, an energetic startup hoping to make education more engaging through interactive books. In order to do this, we also built modern tools for digital publishing.

  1. I led design and development on hundreds of titles from Campbell's Biology to Frommer's Travel Guides.
  2. In my spare time, I prototyped tools to help editors produce interactive books at scale.

When not working, I prefer to be in nature. I am grateful to have had the chance to thru-hike the Pacific Crest Trail, traverse the Sierra High Route, and work on a few farms in Patagonia.

If you’d like to get in touch, shoot me an email. I’m not really on social media these days.
