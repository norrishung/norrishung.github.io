From 2011-2014, I worked at Inkling to help to reimagine the book and publishing system for the digital age.

Positions Held

  • Front-end developer/designer

    2011-2013

  • Manager, Content Platform & Strategy Team

    2013-2014

Building Content

Each digital book was a tight collaboration between a content architect, a technical lead, a design lead, an editorial lead, the publisher, and a production team in India.

Design

My role as a content designer was to translate a diverse range of complex print layouts to responsive web layouts that accurately captured the intent of the content.

Tools used: Illustrator, Photoshop

Coding

As a content developer, I would translate design mocks into HTML/CSS pattern snippet for each type of element in the book. I would work with the design lead to stress-test their layouts and make sure that we both understood how the design would respond across different devices.

I also built various custom javascript based widgets for high-end titles.

Building Tools

I built web tools in my spare time to solve problems. Working on dozens of book over the first year, I began to wonder whether I could do anything to make the process easier for other team members. What started off as hacking projects ended up becoming incredibly useful tools that are still used internally or were incorporated into Inkling's publisher software, Habitat.

Poptip Viewer

Poptip viewer is a tool that visualized every poptip in the book next to the term that it was associated with. This transformed a task that took an entire day to sometime that took less than an hour. This feature was so useful that it was eventually incorporated into Habitat, Inkling’s publishing software.

Traverser

Traverser is a tool that allowed editors to quickly find and check every link in the book. Every time a link was highlighted, a preview of the content it linked to would show up in an adjacent panel. This turned days worth of work to hours.

Sassafras

Sassafrass is a tool to help detect potential problems by providing a bird’s eye view of the CSS code and the frequency at how different selectors were used. This helped to detect many issues in third-party code such as excessive use of important tags, incorrect usage of z-index or absolute positioning, missing browser prefixes, and use of deprecated or experimental CSS selectors.

Building a Platform

In early 2013, Inkling launched Habitat, a publishing platform to allow anyone else to create their own Inkling books. I led the Content Platform and Strategy team to ensure outside content developers could easily build the content they wanted within out platform.

Inkling Base

Essentially a Twitter Bootstrap for digital books, Inkling Base allowed publishers to get started creating their books with 90% of the groundwork already done. It included our ever growing pattern library, template HTML pages, and our SASS codebase with responsive layouts, grid systems, and basic typography already built-in.

Content Conversions

Publishers were eager to begin new projects in Habitat but they also had a huge backlog of titles they wanted to digitize. Our team created flexible SASS design templates as well as custom scripts to convert XML/INDD book files into HTML content. Our team also led the initiative to make our content EPUB3 compatible.

Our team led the project to help Elsevier digitize over 700 titles within half a year. Having never produced more than 50 books at a time previously, this was a huge achievement for the company.

New Content Capabilities

We also focused on collaborating with the engineering teams to enable new capabilities such as LaTeX equation authoring, semantic markup and enrichment (RDFa and Microdata), and incorporating custom javascript widgets.
